Own Problems

  • Find the smallest positive integer n where \( n^2+1 \) is a multiple of 2029.

  • \( ABC \) is a triangle with sides \( AB = 10 \), \( AC = 14 \), \( BC = 16 \). A point \(P\) is chosen uniformly inside \(ABC\).
    What is the probability that \(PA \lt PB\) and \(PA \lt PC\)?

  • Let \( a \), \( b \), \( c \), \( d \), \( e \), \( f \) be positive real numbers such that \[ a + b = d + e, \quad b + c = e + f, \quad c + d = f + a. \] Find the difference between the areas of a triangle with sides \( \sqrt{a^2+ab+b^2} \), \( \sqrt{c^2+cd+d^2} \), and \( \sqrt{e^2+ef+f^2} \).
    and a triangle with sides \( \sqrt{b^2+bc+c^2} \), \( \sqrt{d^2+de+e^2} \), and \( \sqrt{f^2 + fa + a^2} \). (Assume first triangle has larger area.)

  • \( ABC \) is an acute triangle with orthocenter \( H \). Let \( X \) and \( Y \) be points on line \( BC \) where \( AX \) and \( AY \) are the internal and external angle bisector of \( \angle BAC \). Then, let \( P \) and \( Q \) be the intersection of \( (AXY) \) and \( (BHC) \). Prove that one of \( P \) or \( Q \) lies on the \(A\)-median, and the other lies on the \(A\)-altitude.

  • (IMO 1998/3) For any positive integer \( n \), let \( d(n) \) denote the number of positive divisors of \( n \) (including 1 and n itself). Determine all positive integers \( k \) such that \( \frac{d(n^2)}{d(n)} = k \) for some \( n \).
